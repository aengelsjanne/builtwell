01 Who we are

impecable is a small independent studio that designs and builds web and mobile apps for small businesses, operated by Jan Engels. In this policy, "impecable", "we", "us" and "our" refer to that studio.

We are the data controller responsible for the personal data described here. If you have any question about your data or this policy, email us at info@impecable.dev.

02 The short version

This website is deliberately lightweight, and so is our handling of your data:

  • We set no cookies and run no analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking.
  • The contact form does not send anything to a server of ours — it prepares a draft email in your own email app, which you then choose to send.
  • The only thing stored in your browser is your language preference, kept locally on your device.
  • We only ever see the information you deliberately send us — for example, the contents of an email.

The sections below explain this in full.

03 What we collect

Information you send us directly

When you use the contact form on our site, it opens a pre-filled message in your own email client. Nothing is transmitted until you press send in that client. If and when you do email us — via the form or directly — we receive whatever you include, typically your name, email address, and the details of your enquiry. We use this only to reply to you and, where relevant, to scope and discuss a possible project.

Information stored on your device

We save your chosen interface language (for example "en" or "de") in your browser's local storage so the site remembers it on your next visit. This stays on your device, is not personal data, is not sent to us, and you can clear it at any time through your browser settings.

Information collected automatically by our host

Like almost every website, the server that hosts this site may automatically record standard technical request logs — such as your IP address, browser type, and the pages requested — for security and to keep the site running. We do not use these logs to build profiles of visitors.

Web fonts

This site uses Google Fonts to load its typefaces. When a page loads, your browser requests those font files from Google's servers, which means your IP address is transmitted to Google in the process. See Google's privacy policy for how they handle that request.

We do not knowingly collect any special categories of personal data, and we do not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling.

04 How we use it

We use the limited information we receive to:

  • respond to your enquiries and communicate with you;
  • discuss, scope, quote and — if we work together — deliver a project;
  • keep the website secure, available and working correctly;
  • comply with our legal and accounting obligations.

We do not sell your data, and we do not use it to advertise to you.

05 Legal bases

Where the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies, we rely on the following legal bases for processing your data:

  • Consent and/or steps taken at your request prior to entering a contract — when you contact us about working together (Art. 6(1)(a) and (b) GDPR).
  • Legitimate interests — to keep the site secure and to respond to general enquiries (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).
  • Legal obligation — where we are required to retain certain records, for example for tax purposes (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR).

06 Who we share it with

We keep the number of third parties involved to a minimum. We may rely on:

  • our hosting and email providers, who process data on our behalf so the site and our inbox function;
  • Google Fonts, as described above, for delivering typefaces;
  • professional advisers or authorities, where we are legally required to disclose information.

We do not share your personal data with anyone for their own marketing purposes.

07 International transfers

Some of the providers we rely on (such as Google Fonts) may process data on servers located outside your country, including outside the European Economic Area. Where that happens, we expect appropriate safeguards — such as the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses — to be in place to protect your data.

08 How long we keep it

We keep correspondence only for as long as needed to deal with your enquiry and, where a project follows, for the duration of our working relationship and any period we are legally required to retain records afterwards. When data is no longer needed, we delete it. You can ask us to delete your correspondence at any time.

09 Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have some or all of the following rights over your personal data:

  • to access the data we hold about you;
  • to have inaccurate data corrected;
  • to have your data erased;
  • to restrict or object to how we process it;
  • to receive your data in a portable format;
  • to withdraw consent at any time, where we relied on it.

To exercise any of these, just email info@impecable.dev. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

10 Security

This site is served over an encrypted (HTTPS) connection, and we take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect the limited data we hold. No method of transmission or storage is ever completely secure, but because we collect so little, there is very little exposure by design.

11 Children

This website and our services are intended for businesses and adults. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has sent us personal data, contact us and we will delete it.

12 Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time as our site or our practices change. When we do, we will revise the "last updated" date at the top of the page. Significant changes will be reflected here — please check back occasionally.

Questions about your data? Email info@impecable.dev and we'll get back to you.
